# Electric Force

When two charged particles are brought near each other, they exert a force on one another. This is governed by **Coulomb's Law**.

* **Like charges** repel each other (e.g., proton and proton).
* **Unlike charges** attract each other (e.g., proton and electron).

## Coulomb's Law Formula

The magnitude of the electrostatic force ($F$) between two point charges ($q_1$ and $q_2$) is directly proportional to the product of the magnitudes of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance ($r$) between them.

$$F = k \frac{|q_1 \times q_2|}{r^2}$$

Where:
* $F$ is the electric force (Newtons, N)
* $k$ is Coulomb's constant ($\approx 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$)
* $q_1, q_2$ are the amounts of charge (Coulombs, C)
* $r$ is the distance between the centers of the charges (Meters, m)

## Real-World Applications

Electric force isn't just theory; it is utilized in numerous modern technologies:

1.  **Laser Printers & Photocopiers**: Use static electricity and electric force to attract negatively charged toner particles to positively charged areas on a drum, transferring the image to paper.
2.  **Electrostatic Precipitators**: Used in smokestacks to reduce pollution. They impart a negative charge to smoke particles, which are then attracted by electric force to positively charged collection plates.
3.  **Powder Coating / Paint Spraying**: Paint droplets are given a charge as they leave the nozzle, and the object being painted is grounded or given the opposite charge. The electric force ensures an even, efficient coat of paint that wraps around the object.

## Related Problem: Calculating Force

**Problem Statement:**
Two point charges, $q_1 = +2 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C}$ and $q_2 = -3 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C}$, are separated by a distance of $0.05 \text{ m}$. Calculate the electric force between them and state whether it is attractive or repulsive.

**Solution:**
1.  **Identify given values:**
    * $q_1 = 2 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C}$
    * $q_2 = 3 \times 10^{-6} \text{ C}$ (use magnitude for the formula)
    * $r = 0.05 \text{ m}$
    * $k = 8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$

2.  **Apply Coulomb's Law:**
    $$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{(2 \times 10^{-6}) \times (3 \times 10^{-6})}{(0.05)^2}$$
    $$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{6 \times 10^{-12}}{0.0025}$$
    $$F = 21.576 \text{ Newtons}$$

**Conclusion:**
Because $q_1$ is positive and $q_2$ is negative, the forces are **attractive**. The force magnitude is $21.576 \text{ N}$.
