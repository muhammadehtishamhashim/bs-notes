# Fundamental Charge

All matter is made up of atoms, which contain charged particles.

* **Electron ($-e$)**: Has a negative charge of $-1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Proton ($+e$)**: Has a positive charge of $+1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Nucleus**: The central part of the atom is positively charged (contains protons and neutrons).

The formula for total charge is:
$$
\begin{aligned}
Q &= \pm ne
\end{aligned}
$$
*(Where $n$ is an integer and $e$ is the elementary charge)*

## Material Classification

We can classify materials based on their atomic structure, specifically their **valence electrons** (electrons in the outermost shell):

* **Conductor**: Material where valence electrons are **less than 4**.
* **Insulator**: Material where valence electrons are **more than 4**.

## Ionization

**Definition**: Removing or adding an electron from the outermost shell of an atom.

When this process occurs, the material becomes ionized. Let's compare a stable material to an ionized material using our Sodium example.

### 1. Stable Material (Neutral Na)
In a stable, neutral atom, the number of protons equals the number of electrons.
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Net Charge} &= -11e \text{ (electrons)} + 11e \text{ (protons)} \\
&= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

### 2. Ionized Material (Na+)
If the Sodium atom loses its outermost valence electron (1 electron leaves):
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Net Charge} &= +11e \text{ (protons)} - 10e \text{ (remaining electrons)} \\
&= +1e \text{ (net positive charge)}
\end{aligned}
$$
