# Definition of Matrix

According to the Grade 11 Mathematics textbook, a **matrix** is a rectangular array of real numbers (or functions) arranged in rows and columns, enclosed by square brackets `[ ]`. 

The numbers or functions that make up the matrix are called its **elements** or **entries**. Matrices are conventionally denoted by uppercase English alphabets like $A, B, C$, while their elements are denoted by corresponding lowercase letters with double subscripts, like $a_{ij}$.

### Order of a Matrix

The textbook defines the **order** (or dimension) of a matrix by the number of its rows and columns. If a matrix $A$ has $m$ horizontal rows and $n$ vertical columns, it is said to be a matrix of order $m \times n$ (read as "$m$ by $n$").

## Conceptual Questions

### Question 1
Can a matrix have $2.5$ rows or $3.1$ columns? Explain based on the textbook definition.

**Solution:**
No. According to the textbook definition of a matrix's order ($m \times n$), the variables $m$ (rows) and $n$ (columns) must be strictly positive integers (natural numbers). A fractional row or column is mathematically meaningless.

### Question 2
If a matrix has exactly $7$ elements, what are its possible orders? Why?

**Solution:**
The total elements in a matrix equals $m \times n = 7$. Since $7$ is a prime number, the only natural numbers that multiply to $7$ are $1$ and $7$. Therefore, the only possible orders are $1 \times 7$ and $7 \times 1$.

### Question 3
What is the difference between the element $a_{12}$ and the element $a_{21}$ in a matrix?

**Solution:**
The element $a_{12}$ is located in the 1st row and the 2nd column. The element $a_{21}$ is located in the 2nd row and the 1st column. The subscripts dictate the exact spatial address within the rectangular array.

## Exercise Questions

### Question 1
Find the order of the matrix $A$ and determine the value of the element $a_{23}$.
$$A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 5 & -1 & 7 \\ 3 & 0 & 4 & 9 \\ -5 & 6 & 1 & 8 \end{bmatrix}$$

**Solution:**
The number of horizontal rows ($m$) is $3$ and vertical columns ($n$) is $4$. Therefore, the order of matrix $A$ is $3 \times 4$. The element $a_{23}$ is located in the 2nd row and 3rd column, which gives $a_{23} = 4$.

### Question 2
If a matrix has 12 elements, what are all the possible orders it can have?

**Solution:**
Let the order be $m \times n$. The total elements must equal $12$. We must find all natural number pairs $(m, n)$ whose product is 12:
- $1 \times 12$
- $12 \times 1$
- $2 \times 6$
- $6 \times 2$
- $3 \times 4$
- $4 \times 3$

### Question 3
Construct a $2 \times 2$ matrix $A = [a_{ij}]$ whose elements are given by the formula $a_{ij} = \frac{(i + 2j)^2}{2}$.

The general form of a $2 \times 2$ matrix is $A = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} \end{bmatrix}$. We calculate each element by substituting the row index $i$ and column index $j$ into the given formula.

<MathWrapper title="Element Calculations">

$$
\begin{aligned}
a_{11} &= \frac{(1 + 2(1))^2}{2} = \frac{3^2}{2} = \frac{9}{2} \\
a_{12} &= \frac{(1 + 2(2))^2}{2} = \frac{5^2}{2} = \frac{25}{2} \\
a_{21} &= \frac{(2 + 2(1))^2}{2} = \frac{4^2}{2} = \frac{16}{2} = 8 \\
a_{22} &= \frac{(2 + 2(2))^2}{2} = \frac{6^2}{2} = \frac{36}{2} = 18 \\
A &= \begin{bmatrix} \frac{9}{2} & \frac{25}{2} \\ 8 & 18 \end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 4
Construct a $3 \times 2$ matrix $B = [b_{ij}]$ where the elements are defined as $b_{ij} = |2i - 3j|$.

The matrix $B$ will have 3 rows and 2 columns. We calculate the absolute value for each specific row ($i$) and column ($j$) coordinate.

<MathWrapper title="Element Calculations">

$$
\begin{aligned}
b_{11} &= |2(1) - 3(1)| = |-1| = 1 \\
b_{12} &= |2(1) - 3(2)| = |-4| = 4 \\
b_{21} &= |2(2) - 3(1)| = |1| = 1 \\
b_{22} &= |2(2) - 3(2)| = |-2| = 2 \\
b_{31} &= |2(3) - 3(1)| = |3| = 3 \\
b_{32} &= |2(3) - 3(2)| = |0| = 0 \\
B &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 5
Determine the general form of a matrix of order $1 \times 4$ and write down the total number of elements.

**Solution:**
A $1 \times 4$ matrix has exactly 1 row and 4 columns. 
Let the matrix be $C$. The general form is: $C = \begin{bmatrix} c_{11} & c_{12} & c_{13} & c_{14} \end{bmatrix}$.
The total number of elements is $1 \times 4 = 4$.

### Question 6
Write the element $a_{32}$ of the matrix $A = [a_{ij}]_{3 \times 3}$ given by the formula $a_{ij} = e^{i-j} \sin(j\pi)$.

**Solution:**
We need to find the element in the 3rd row, 2nd column ($i=3, j=2$). Substituting these into the formula yields:
$$a_{32} = e^{3-2} \sin(2\pi) = e^1 \times 0 = 0$$

### Question 7
Construct a $2 \times 3$ matrix $X = [x_{ij}]$ given by the formula $x_{ij} = i^2 - j^2$.

We will substitute the $i$ and $j$ values for all 6 coordinates in the $2 \times 3$ matrix.

<MathWrapper title="Element Calculations">

$$
\begin{aligned}
x_{11} &= 1^2 - 1^2 = 0 \\
x_{12} &= 1^2 - 2^2 = -3 \\
x_{13} &= 1^2 - 3^2 = -8 \\
x_{21} &= 2^2 - 1^2 = 3 \\
x_{22} &= 2^2 - 2^2 = 0 \\
x_{23} &= 2^2 - 3^2 = -5 \\
X &= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -3 & -8 \\ 3 & 0 & -5 \end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 8
If a matrix has 8 elements, what are all the possible orders it can have?

**Solution:**
We must find all integer pairs that multiply to 8. The possible combinations are $1 \times 8$, $8 \times 1$, $2 \times 4$, and $4 \times 2$.

### Question 9
Construct a $3 \times 3$ matrix $I = [i_{jk}]$ where $i_{jk} = 1$ if $j = k$, and $i_{jk} = 0$ if $j \neq k$.

Based on the condition, any element on the main diagonal (where row equals column, meaning $j=k$) is $1$. Any element outside the diagonal is $0$.

<MathWrapper title="Matrix Construction">

$$
\begin{aligned}
I &= \begin{bmatrix} i_{11} & i_{12} & i_{13} \\ i_{21} & i_{22} & i_{23} \\ i_{31} & i_{32} & i_{33} \end{bmatrix} \\
I &= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 10
Identify the row index and column index of the number $15$ in the matrix below:
$$M = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 6 & 9 \\ 12 & 15 & 18 \\ 21 & 24 & 27 \end{bmatrix}$$

**Solution:**
The number $15$ is located in the second horizontal row ($i=2$). It is also located in the second vertical column ($j=2$). Therefore, its elemental notation is $m_{22}$.
