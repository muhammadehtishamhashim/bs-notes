# Determinant of a Square Matrix

Every square matrix $A$ of order $n \times n$ is associated with a specific real number known as its **determinant**. The determinant of a matrix $A$ is denoted by $|A|$ or $\text{det}(A)$. 

It is important to note that determinants are **only defined for square matrices**.

### Determinant of a $2 \times 2$ Matrix

For a $2 \times 2$ matrix, the determinant is calculated by taking the product of the main diagonal elements and subtracting the product of the secondary diagonal elements.

If $A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}$, then its determinant is given by:

$$|A| = \begin{vmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{vmatrix} = ad - bc$$

### Singular vs Non-Singular Matrices

According to the textbook:
* **Singular Matrix**: A square matrix $A$ is called singular if its determinant is strictly equal to zero ($|A| = 0$).
* **Non-Singular Matrix**: A square matrix $A$ is called non-singular if its determinant is not equal to zero ($|A| \neq 0$).

## Conceptual Questions

### Question 1
Can we calculate the determinant of a $2 \times 3$ matrix? Explain based on textbook principles.

**Solution:**
No. Determinants are strictly defined only for square matrices (where the number of rows equals the number of columns, such as $2 \times 2$ or $3 \times 3$). A $2 \times 3$ matrix is rectangular, so it does not possess a determinant.

### Question 2
If the determinant of a matrix is a negative number, does that make it a singular matrix?

**Solution:**
No. A singular matrix is defined strictly by the condition $|A| = 0$. If the determinant is any non-zero value, whether positive or negative, the matrix is classified as non-singular.

## Exercise Questions

### Question 1
Evaluate the determinant of the matrix $A = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$.

We will use the cross-multiplication formula for a $2 \times 2$ matrix, subtracting the secondary diagonal from the main diagonal.

<MathWrapper title="Evaluating Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|A| &= (5)(4) - (2)(-3) \\
|A| &= 20 - (-6) \\
|A| &= 20 + 6 \\
|A| &= 26
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 2
Find the value of $|B|$ if $B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 7 \\ -2 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$.

Apply the determinant expansion.

<MathWrapper title="Evaluating Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|B| &= (0)(5) - (7)(-2) \\
|B| &= 0 - (-14) \\
|B| &= 14
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 3
Calculate the determinant of the matrix $C = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -1 \\ 8 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$. Is matrix $C$ singular or non-singular?

We evaluate the determinant first to check its singularity.

<MathWrapper title="Singularity Check">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|C| &= (-4)(2) - (-1)(8) \\
|C| &= -8 - (-8) \\
|C| &= -8 + 8 \\
|C| &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

Since $|C| = 0$, the matrix $C$ is a **singular matrix**.

### Question 4
Evaluate the determinant of the identity matrix $I_2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$.

Expand the determinant using the standard formula.

<MathWrapper title="Evaluating Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|I_2| &= (1)(1) - (0)(0) \\
|I_2| &= 1 - 0 \\
|I_2| &= 1
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 5
Find the value of $x$ if the matrix $M = \begin{bmatrix} x & 3 \\ 4 & 6 \end{bmatrix}$ is a singular matrix.

If the matrix is singular, its determinant must be exactly zero. We set up the equation and solve for $x$.

<MathWrapper title="Solving for Unknown">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|M| &= 0 \\
(x)(6) - (3)(4) &= 0 \\
6x - 12 &= 0 \\
6x &= 12 \\
x &= 2
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 6
Find the value of $y$ if the determinant of the matrix $P = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & y \\ -1 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$ is equal to $17$.

We equate the determinant expression to $17$ and isolate $y$.

<MathWrapper title="Solving for Unknown">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|P| &= 17 \\
(2)(5) - (y)(-1) &= 17 \\
10 + y &= 17 \\
y &= 17 - 10 \\
y &= 7
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 7
Solve for $x$ given the equation $\begin{vmatrix} x & 4 \\ 2 & x \end{vmatrix} = 0$.

Expand the determinant on the left-hand side to form a quadratic equation.

<MathWrapper title="Solving Quadratic Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
(x)(x) - (4)(2) &= 0 \\
x^2 - 8 &= 0 \\
x^2 &= 8 \\
x &= \pm \sqrt{8} \\
x &= \pm 2\sqrt{2}
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 8
Check if the matrix $D = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{1}{2} & 2 \\ \frac{1}{4} & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ is singular or non-singular.

We calculate the determinant of the fractional matrix.

<MathWrapper title="Evaluating Fractional Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|D| &= \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)(1) - (2)\left(\frac{1}{4}\right) \\
|D| &= \frac{1}{2} - \frac{2}{4} \\
|D| &= \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2} \\
|D| &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

Since the determinant is $0$, the matrix $D$ is **singular**.

### Question 9
Evaluate the determinant involving trigonometric functions: $A = \begin{bmatrix} \cos \theta & -\sin \theta \\ \sin \theta & \cos \theta \end{bmatrix}$.

Expand the determinant using the cross-multiplication formula, then apply trigonometric identities.

<MathWrapper title="Trigonometric Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|A| &= (\cos \theta)(\cos \theta) - (-\sin \theta)(\sin \theta) \\
|A| &= \cos^2 \theta - (-\sin^2 \theta) \\
|A| &= \cos^2 \theta + \sin^2 \theta
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

Using the fundamental trigonometric identity $\cos^2 \theta + \sin^2 \theta = 1$, we get $|A| = 1$.

### Question 10
If $A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$, find the determinant of the scalar multiplied matrix $|2A|$.

First, we must multiply the matrix $A$ by the scalar $2$, then find the determinant of the new matrix.

<MathWrapper title="Determinant of Scaled Matrix">

$$
\begin{aligned}
2A &= 2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 2 \\ 4 & 8 \end{bmatrix} \\
|2A| &= (6)(8) - (2)(4) \\
|2A| &= 48 - 8 \\
|2A| &= 40
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>
