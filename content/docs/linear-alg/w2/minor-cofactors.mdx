# Minors and Cofactors

To calculate the determinant of a $3 \times 3$ matrix (or any higher-order matrix), the textbook introduces two foundational concepts: **Minors** and **Cofactors**.

### 1. Minor of an Element ($M_{ij}$)

The minor of an element $a_{ij}$ in a square matrix is the determinant of the smaller sub-matrix that remains after **deleting the $i$-th row and the $j$-th column**. It is denoted by $M_{ij}$.

### 2. Cofactor of an Element ($C_{ij}$)

The cofactor of an element $a_{ij}$ is simply its minor $M_{ij}$ multiplied by a positional sign. The textbook defines it with the formula:

$$C_{ij} = (-1)^{i+j} M_{ij}$$

The positional sign $(-1)^{i+j}$ creates an alternating checkerboard pattern of plus and minus signs across the matrix:
$$ \begin{bmatrix} + & - & + \\ - & + & - \\ + & - & + \end{bmatrix} $$

### 3. Expansion of a $3 \times 3$ Determinant

The determinant of a $3 \times 3$ matrix can be evaluated by expanding along **any** row or **any** column. The result is the sum of the products of the elements of that row/column and their corresponding cofactors.

Expanding along the first row ($R_1$):
$$|A| = a_{11}C_{11} + a_{12}C_{12} + a_{13}C_{13}$$

Or, writing it out fully with minors:
$$|A| = a_{11}(M_{11}) - a_{12}(M_{12}) + a_{13}(M_{13})$$

## Conceptual Questions

### Question 1
If you evaluate a $3 \times 3$ determinant by expanding along Row 1, and then evaluate the same matrix by expanding along Column 2, will you get the same answer?

**Solution:**
Yes. According to Laplace's formula in the textbook, the determinant of a matrix is a unique characteristic scalar value. Expanding along any row or any column will yield the exact same final result.

### Question 2
What is the positional sign for the element located at $a_{32}$ when finding its cofactor?

**Solution:**
The positional sign is calculated using $(-1)^{i+j}$. For the element $a_{32}$, $i=3$ and $j=2$. 
$(-1)^{3+2} = (-1)^5 = -1$. The sign is negative.

## Exercise Questions

### Question 1
Given the matrix $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$, find the minor $M_{21}$.

To find $M_{21}$, we delete the 2nd row and 1st column, leaving a $2 \times 2$ determinant.

<MathWrapper title="Calculating Minor">

$$
\begin{aligned}
M_{21} &= \begin{vmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 8 & 9 \end{vmatrix} \\
M_{21} &= (3)(9) - (1)(8) \\
M_{21} &= 27 - 8 \\
M_{21} &= 19
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 2
Using the same matrix $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 3 & 1 \\ 4 & 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 & 9 \end{bmatrix}$, find the minor $M_{33}$.

Delete the 3rd row and 3rd column.

<MathWrapper title="Calculating Minor">

$$
\begin{aligned}
M_{33} &= \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 3 \\ 4 & 5 \end{vmatrix} \\
M_{33} &= (2)(5) - (3)(4) \\
M_{33} &= 10 - 12 \\
M_{33} &= -2
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 3
For the matrix $B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$, find the cofactor $C_{12}$.

We first find the minor $M_{12}$ (delete row 1, column 2), then apply the positional sign.

<MathWrapper title="Calculating Cofactor">

$$
\begin{aligned}
M_{12} &= \begin{vmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 5 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = (0)(-1) - (1)(5) = -5 \\
C_{12} &= (-1)^{1+2} M_{12} \\
C_{12} &= (-1)^3 (-5) \\
C_{12} &= (-1)(-5) \\
C_{12} &= 5
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 4
For the matrix $B = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -2 & 4 \\ 0 & 3 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 & -1 \end{bmatrix}$, find the cofactor $C_{22}$.

Find $M_{22}$ (delete row 2, col 2), then apply the sign.

<MathWrapper title="Calculating Cofactor">

$$
\begin{aligned}
M_{22} &= \begin{vmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 5 & -1 \end{vmatrix} = (1)(-1) - (4)(5) = -1 - 20 = -21 \\
C_{22} &= (-1)^{2+2} M_{22} \\
C_{22} &= (-1)^4 (-21) \\
C_{22} &= (1)(-21) \\
C_{22} &= -21
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 5
Evaluate the determinant of $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 & 3 \\ 4 & -1 & 0 \\ 5 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ by expanding along Row 1.

We apply the formula $|A| = a_{11}(M_{11}) - a_{12}(M_{12}) + a_{13}(M_{13})$.

<MathWrapper title="Determinant Expansion">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|A| &= 2 \begin{vmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} - 1 \begin{vmatrix} 4 & 0 \\ 5 & 1 \end{vmatrix} + 3 \begin{vmatrix} 4 & -1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} \\
|A| &= 2((-1)(1) - (0)(2)) - 1((4)(1) - (0)(5)) + 3((4)(2) - (-1)(5)) \\
|A| &= 2(-1 - 0) - 1(4 - 0) + 3(8 + 5) \\
|A| &= 2(-1) - 1(4) + 3(13) \\
|A| &= -2 - 4 + 39 \\
|A| &= 33
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 6
Evaluate the determinant of the same matrix $A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 & 3 \\ 4 & -1 & 0 \\ 5 & 2 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ by expanding along Column 3 to verify the result.

The positional signs for Column 3 are $+, -, +$.

<MathWrapper title="Column Expansion">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|A| &= a_{13}C_{13} + a_{23}C_{23} + a_{33}C_{33} \\
|A| &= 3 \begin{vmatrix} 4 & -1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - 0 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} + 1 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 4 & -1 \end{vmatrix} \\
|A| &= 3((4)(2) - (-1)(5)) - 0 + 1((2)(-1) - (1)(4)) \\
|A| &= 3(8 + 5) + 1(-2 - 4) \\
|A| &= 3(13) + 1(-6) \\
|A| &= 39 - 6 \\
|A| &= 33
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

The result is $33$, proving expansion works along any column.

### Question 7
Find the value of $x$ if the matrix $M = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & x & 3 \\ 0 & 2 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$ is singular.

A singular matrix has a determinant of $0$. We expand along Column 1 because it has a zero, making calculation easier.

<MathWrapper title="Solving Singular Matrix">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|M| &= 0 \\
1 \begin{vmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - 0 + 1 \begin{vmatrix} x & 3 \\ 2 & 1 \end{vmatrix} &= 0 \\
1(4 - 0) + 1(x(1) - 3(2)) &= 0 \\
4 + (x - 6) &= 0 \\
x - 2 &= 0 \\
x &= 2
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

### Question 8
Solve for $x$ given the equation $\begin{vmatrix} 2 & 0 & x \\ 1 & x & 2 \\ 3 & 1 & 1 \end{vmatrix} = 0$.

We expand the determinant along Row 1 to find the quadratic equation.

<MathWrapper title="Solving Quadratic Equation">

$$
\begin{aligned}
2 \begin{vmatrix} x & 2 \\ 1 & 1 \end{vmatrix} - 0 + x \begin{vmatrix} 1 & x \\ 3 & 1 \end{vmatrix} &= 0 \\
2(x(1) - 2(1)) + x(1(1) - x(3)) &= 0 \\
2(x - 2) + x(1 - 3x) &= 0 \\
2x - 4 + x - 3x^2 &= 0 \\
-3x^2 + 3x - 4 &= 0 \\
3x^2 - 3x + 4 &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

Using the quadratic formula where $a=3, b=-3, c=4$:
$x = \frac{-(-3) \pm \sqrt{(-3)^2 - 4(3)(4)}}{2(3)} = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{9 - 48}}{6} = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{-39}}{6}$
Since the discriminant is negative, $x$ has no real roots (complex roots only).

### Question 9
Evaluate the determinant of an Upper Triangular Matrix $U = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 5 & 2 \\ 0 & 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 0 & 2 \end{bmatrix}$.

Expanding along Column 1 is highly efficient because it contains two zeros.

<MathWrapper title="Triangular Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|U| &= 4 \begin{vmatrix} 3 & -1 \\ 0 & 2 \end{vmatrix} - 0 + 0 \\
|U| &= 4((3)(2) - (-1)(0)) \\
|U| &= 4(6 - 0) \\
|U| &= 24
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

*(Notice the determinant is simply the product of its main diagonal: $4 \times 3 \times 2 = 24$.)*

### Question 10
If all the elements of Row 2 in a $3 \times 3$ matrix $A$ are completely zero, calculate its determinant.
$$A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b & c \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \\ d & e & f \end{bmatrix}$$

If we expand the determinant along the row of zeros (Row 2), every cofactor is multiplied by zero.

<MathWrapper title="Zero Row Determinant">

$$
\begin{aligned}
|A| &= -0 \cdot M_{21} + 0 \cdot M_{22} - 0 \cdot M_{23} \\
|A| &= 0 + 0 - 0 \\
|A| &= 0
\end{aligned}
$$

</MathWrapper>

Thus, any matrix with a complete row or column of zeros is always singular ($|A|=0$).
