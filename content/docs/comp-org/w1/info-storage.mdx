## How Computers Store Information

Computers are electronic devices that operate in two states: **ON** (high voltage) and **OFF** (low voltage). We represent these states mathematically using the binary system.

* **Bit (Binary Digit):** The smallest unit of data (0 or 1).
* **Byte:** A group of 8 bits. This is the standard unit for addressing memory.

### The Power of Abstraction
We don't usually read raw bits. We group them to represent larger numbers, characters, or instructions.

### Why 8 bits?
Historically, 8 bits were sufficient to store a single character of text (English alphabet, numbers, and punctuation) using the ASCII encoding standard.
