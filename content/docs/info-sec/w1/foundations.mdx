# Introduction to Information Security

**Information Security (InfoSec)** at its core is simply the practice of **making data secure**. It protects physical and electronic information from unauthorized access, modification, or destruction.

### The Basic Encryption Flow

A fundamental method to secure information is **Encryption**. This process involves transforming readable data into an unreadable format before transmission over a medium.

## Mathematical Foundations of Cryptography

To implement strong protocols and encryption, we rely heavily on Number Theory. The core mathematical topics covered in modern cryptography include:

* **Divisibility:** Operations where the remainder is $0$ (uses `%` modulo operator).
* **Greatest Common Divisor (GCD):** Finding the largest shared factor.
* **Euclidean Algorithm:** An efficient method to compute the GCD.
* **Prime Numbers:** The building blocks of encryption keys.
* **Congruence & Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT):** Advanced modular arithmetic.
* **Group Theory:** Algebraic structures used in modern cyphers.

## Prime Numbers

A **Prime Number** is a number greater than $1$ that has exactly two factors: **$1$ and itself**. It cannot be divided further by any other number without leaving a remainder.

### Prime Factorization Examples

Every number is either a prime or can be broken down into prime factors. 

| Number | Prime Factorization | Is Prime? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **10** | $2^1 \times 5^1$ (Factors: $2, 5$) | **No** |
| **11** | $1 \times 11^1$ (Factors: $1, 11$) | <span className="text-green-500 font-bold">Yes</span> |
| **37** | $1 \times 37^1$ (Factors: $1, 37$) | <span className="text-green-500 font-bold">Yes</span> |
| **100** | $2^2 \times 5^2$ (Factors: $2, 5$) | **No** |
| **308** | $2^2 \times 7^1 \times 11^1$ (Factors: $2, 7, 11$) | **No** |
