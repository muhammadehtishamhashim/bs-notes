# Divisibility Theory and GCD

Before learning complex encryption algorithms, we must master the basics of how numbers divide into one another.

## Divisibility

In cryptography and number theory, **Divisibility** strictly refers to a division operation where the **remainder equals exactly 0**. 

In programming, this is heavily reliant on the modulo operator (`%`), which returns the remainder of a division. If `a % b == 0`, then $a$ is divisible by $b$.

An integer $a$ is said to be divisible by an integer $b$ (where $b \neq 0$) if there exists some integer $q$ such that:
<MathWrapper title="Divisibility Condition">
$$ a = b \cdot q $$
</MathWrapper>

---

## Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) / HCF

The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD), also known as the Highest Common Factor (HCF), is the largest positive integer that perfectly divides two or more numbers without leaving a remainder.

Here are three basic methods to find the GCD before moving to advanced algorithms.

### Method 1: Listing Factors
The simplest way is to list all factors and find the highest common one.
**Example: Find GCD of 12 and 33**
* Factors of $12 = 1, 2, \mathbf{3}, 4, 6, 12$
* Factors of $33 = 1, \mathbf{3}, 11, 33$
* **Result:** $\text{GCD}(12, 33) = \mathbf{3}$

### Method 2: Prime Factorization
Break both numbers down into their prime components and multiply the shared primes.
**Example: Find GCD of 25 and 150**
* $25 = \mathbf{5 \times 5}$
* $150 = 2 \times 3 \times \mathbf{5 \times 5}$
* Common Primes $= 5 \times 5$
* **Result:** $\text{GCD}(25, 150) = \mathbf{25}$

### Method 3: Prime Number Rule (Coprimes)
If both numbers provided are prime numbers, they share no common factors other than 1.
**Example: Find GCD of 13 and 31**
* 13 is Prime.
* 31 is Prime.
* **Result:** $\text{GCD}(13, 31) = \mathbf{1}$

<Callout title="Coprime Numbers" type="info">
Whenever the $\text{GCD}$ of two numbers is exactly $1$, those numbers are referred to as **Coprime** or mutually prime. They do not have to be prime numbers themselves (e.g., $8$ and $9$ are coprime because $\text{GCD}(8,9) = 1$).
</Callout>

## Comprehensive Summary
* **What it is:** Divisibility theory explores the relationships of integers under division where no remainder exists. 
* **Core Concept:** The Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) is the absolute largest integer that divides two numbers perfectly. 
* **Why it matters:** Establishing whether two numbers are coprime ($\text{GCD} = 1$) is a mandatory prerequisite for generating cryptographic keys and calculating modular inverses later on.
